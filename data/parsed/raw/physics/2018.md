### Q6.
Two electric bulbs P and Q have their resistances in the ratio of 1 : 2. They are connected in series across a battery. Find the ratio of the power dissipation in these bulbs.

**Answer:** $P_P : P_Q = 1 : 2$

**Solution:**
Given:
$$\frac{R_P}{R_Q} = \frac{1}{2}$$

Since the bulbs are connected in **series**, the current through both is the same:
$$I_P = I_Q = I$$

Power dissipated:
$$P = I^2 R$$

Therefore:
$$\frac{P_P}{P_Q} = \frac{I_P^2 \, R_P}{I_Q^2 \, R_Q} = \frac{R_P}{R_Q} = \boxed{\frac{1}{2}}$$

### Q8.
**(a)** Why are infra-red waves often called heat waves? Explain.

**Answer:**

**Solution:** Infrared waves are called heat waves because:
- They are readily absorbed by most materials and produce a **heating effect**.
- They are **emitted by hot bodies** (all objects above absolute zero emit infrared radiation).
- When absorbed by matter, they increase the kinetic energy of atoms/molecules, raising the temperature.

---

**(b)** What do you understand by the statement, "Electromagnetic waves transport momentum"?

**Answer:**

**Solution:** When electromagnetic waves strike a body, they transfer **momentum** to the body. Although EM waves have no mass, they carry momentum given by:
$$p = \frac{U}{c}$$
where $U$ is the energy of the wave and $c$ is the speed of light. Upon hitting an object, this momentum is transferred from the EM wave to the body (the wave exerts radiation pressure). Thus, EM waves **transport momentum** even without transporting mass.

$$F = F' + F_3 = \frac{Q}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\left[\frac{\sqrt{2}\,q}{a^2} + \frac{Q}{2a^2}\right]$$

### Q12.

**(a)** Define the term 'conductivity' of a metallic wire. Write its SI unit.

**Answer:**

**Solution:**
From $R = \dfrac{\rho\,\ell}{A}$, resistivity is:
$$\rho = \frac{R\,A}{\ell}$$

**Conductivity** ($\sigma$) is defined as the **reciprocal of resistivity**:
$$\sigma = \frac{1}{\rho} = \frac{\ell}{R\cdot A}$$

**SI unit:** Siemens per metre $(\text{S\,m}^{-1})$ or mho/m $(\mho\,\text{m}^{-1})$

---

**(b)** Using the concept of free electrons in a conductor, derive the expression for the conductivity of a wire in terms of number density and relaxation time. Hence obtain the relation between current density and the applied electric field $E$.

**Answer:**

**Solution:**

From Ohm's law and definitions:
$$R = \frac{\rho\,\ell}{A}, \quad V = IR \implies V = \frac{I\,\rho\,\ell}{A}$$

Current density:
$$j = \frac{I}{A}$$

Electric field:
$$E = \frac{V}{\ell}$$

Therefore:
$$E = j\,\rho \implies j = \frac{E}{\rho} = \sigma E$$

$$\boxed{j = \sigma E}$$

**Microscopic derivation:**

An electron in electric field $E$ experiences force $F = eE$, giving acceleration:
$$a = \frac{eE}{m}$$

The drift velocity (using $v = u + at$, with average time between collisions = relaxation time $\tau$):
$$|v_d| = \frac{eE}{m}\tau$$

Current through cross-section of area $A$:
$$I \cdot \Delta t = ne\,A\,v_d\,\Delta t$$

Current density:
$$j = \frac{I}{A} = ne\,v_d = ne\cdot\frac{eE}{m}\tau = \frac{ne^2\tau}{m}E$$

Therefore:
$$\boxed{j = \frac{ne^2\tau}{m}\,E}$$

Comparing with $j = \sigma E$:
$$\boxed{\sigma = \frac{ne^2\tau}{m}}$$

where $n$ = number density of free electrons, $e$ = electron charge, $\tau$ = relaxation time, $m$ = electron mass.

- $f$ = focal length of (lens + liquid lens) system = $x$ (with liquid)
- $f_1$ = focal length of glass lens alone = $y$ (without liquid, mirror acts as plane reflector)
- $f_2$ = focal length of plano-concave liquid lens

$$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{f_1} + \frac{1}{f_2}$$

$$\frac{1}{f_2} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{f_1} = \frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{y}$$

$$\mu' = \frac{y}{x} - 1 + 1$$

Revisiting sign convention carefully:

$$\frac{x - y}{x} = \mu' - 1 \implies \mu' = 1 + \frac{x-y}{x} = 2 - \frac{y}{x}$$

### Q18.

**(a)** State Bohr's postulates to define stable orbits in hydrogen atom. How does de Broglie's hypothesis explain the stability of these orbits?

**Answer:**

**Solution:**

**Bohr's Postulates:**

1. **Orbital postulate:** Electrons revolve in certain fixed circular orbits (stationary states) around the nucleus without radiating energy. The necessary centripetal force is provided by the electrostatic (Coulomb) attraction between the electron and the proton:
$$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{e^2}{r^2}$$

2. **Quantisation postulate:** An electron can revolve only in those orbits for which the angular momentum is an integral multiple of $\dfrac{h}{2\pi}$:
$$L = mvr = \frac{nh}{2\pi}, \quad n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$$

3. **Radiation postulate:** When an electron jumps from a higher energy orbit ($n$th) to a lower energy orbit ($p$th), it emits a photon of energy:
$$E_n - E_p = h\nu$$

**De Broglie's Explanation:**

According to de Broglie, every moving particle has an associated wavelength:
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$

For a stable orbit, the electron wave must form a **standing wave** — the circumference of the orbit must be an integral multiple of the de Broglie wavelength:
$$n\lambda = 2\pi r$$

$$n\cdot\frac{h}{mv} = 2\pi r$$

$$nh = 2\pi\,mv\,r \implies mvr = \frac{nh}{2\pi}$$

This is exactly Bohr's quantisation condition. Thus, de Broglie's hypothesis provides a physical basis for Bohr's postulate — only those orbits are stable in which the electron wave interferes constructively with itself (standing wave condition).

[Diagram: Circular orbit of radius $r$ showing wavelengths $\lambda$ fitting exactly around the circumference as a standing wave.]

**(b)** A hydrogen atom initially in the ground state absorbs a photon which excites it to the $n = 4$ level. Estimate the frequency of the photon.

**Answer:** $\nu \approx 2.46 \times 10^{15}\ \text{Hz}$

**Solution:**
Energy of ground state ($n = 1$): $E_1 = -13.6\ \text{eV}$

Energy of $n = 4$ level: $E_4 = -\dfrac{13.6}{16}\ \text{eV} = -0.85\ \text{eV}$

Energy of absorbed photon:
$$h\nu = E_4 - E_1 = -0.85 - (-13.6) = 12.75\ \text{eV}$$

$$h\nu = 12.75 \times 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{J}$$

### Q22.

**(a)** Give three reasons why modulation of a message signal is necessary for long distance transmission.

**Answer:**

**Solution:** Modulation is necessary because:

1. **Size/Height of Antenna:** For efficient transmission, the length of the antenna should be comparable to the wavelength ($\sim \lambda/4$). 3. **Avoidance of Mixing / Multiplexing (Utilisation of Frequency Spectrum):** Multiple audio signals can be transmitted simultaneously by assigning each a different carrier frequency (frequency division multiplexing), preventing interference between different broadcasts.

**(b)** Explain with an example, how power loss is reduced if the energy is transmitted over long distances as an alternating current rather than a direct current.

**Solution:**
Power loss in transmission lines is given by:
$$P_{\text{loss}} = I^2 R = \frac{P^2 R}{V^2}$$

where $P$ is the power transmitted, $R$ is the resistance of the line, and $V$ is the transmission voltage.

If transmitted at $V = 100\ \text{kV}$:
$$I = 100\ \text{A}, \quad P_{\text{loss}} = (100)^2 \times 10 = 10^5\ \text{W} = 0.1\ \text{MW}$$

Power loss is reduced 100-fold by increasing voltage 10-fold.
