### Q1.
The area of a triangle formed by vertices O, A and B, where $\overrightarrow{OA} = \hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}$ and $\overrightarrow{OB} = -3\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + \hat{k}$ is:

(a) $3\sqrt{5}$ sq. units
(b) $5\sqrt{5}$ sq. units
(c) $6\sqrt{5}$ sq. units
(d) $4$ sq. units

**Answer:** (a) $3\sqrt{5}$ sq. units

**Solution:**

The area of a triangle with two sides given as vectors is $\dfrac{1}{2}|\vec{A} \times \vec{B}|$.

$$\frac{1}{2}\left|\overrightarrow{OA} \times \overrightarrow{OB}\right| = \frac{1}{2}\begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ -3 & -2 & 1 \end{vmatrix}$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}\left|\hat{i}\{(2)(1)-(-2)(3)\} - \hat{j}\{(1)(1)-(-3)(3)\} + \hat{k}\{(1)(-2)-(-3)(2)\}\right|$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}\left|\hat{i}(2+6) - \hat{j}(1+9) + \hat{k}(-2+6)\right|$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}\left|8\hat{i} - 10\hat{j} + 4\hat{k}\right|$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{64 + 100 + 16} = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{180} = \frac{1}{2} \times 6\sqrt{5} = 3\sqrt{5}$$

Therefore, the area is $3\sqrt{5}$ sq. units.

### Q2.
If $\cos\!\left(\sin^{-1}\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{5}} + \cos^{-1}x\right) = 0$, then $x$ is equal to:

(a) $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{5}}$
(b) $-\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{5}}$
(c) $\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{5}}$
(d) $1$

**Answer:** (c) $\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{5}}$

**Solution:**

Given: $\cos\!\left(\sin^{-1}\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{5}} + \cos^{-1}x\right) = 0$

Applying $\cos^{-1}$ to both sides:

$$\sin^{-1}\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} + \cos^{-1}x = \cos^{-1}(0) = \frac{\pi}{2}$$

Using $\cos^{-1}x = \dfrac{\pi}{2} - \sin^{-1}x$:

$$\sin^{-1}\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} + \frac{\pi}{2} - \sin^{-1}x = \frac{\pi}{2}$$

$$\sin^{-1}x = \sin^{-1}\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}$$

$$\therefore x = \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}$$

### Q4.
The function $f(x) = \dfrac{x-1}{x(x^2-1)}$ is discontinuous at:

(a) exactly one point
(b) exactly two points
(c) exactly three points
(d) no point

**Answer:** (c) exactly three points

**Solution:**

Rewrite: $f(x) = \dfrac{x-1}{x(x-1)(x+1)}$

The function is discontinuous when the denominator is zero:

$$x(x-1)(x+1) = 0 \implies x = 0,\ x = 1,\ x = -1$$

Therefore, the function is discontinuous at exactly **three points**.

The coordinates of the foot of the perpendicular drawn from the point $(2, -3, 4)$ on the $y$-axis is:

### Q7.
The relation $R$ in the set $\{1, 2, 3\}$ given by $R = \{(1,2),(2,1),(1,1)\}$ is:

(a) symmetric and transitive, but not reflexive
(b) reflexive and symmetric, but not transitive
(c) symmetric, but neither reflexive nor transitive
(d) an equivalence relation

**Answer:** (a) symmetric and transitive, but not reflexive

**Solution:**

- **Reflexive:** $(2,2)$ and $(3,3)$ are not in $R$. So $R$ is **not reflexive**.
- **Symmetric:** $(1,2) \in R$ and $(2,1) \in R$ ✓. So $R$ is **symmetric**.
- **Transitive:** $(1,2) \in R$, $(2,1) \in R$, and $(1,1) \in R$ ✓. So $R$ is **transitive**.

Hence, $R$ is symmetric and transitive, but **not reflexive**.

### Q8.
The angle between the vectors $\hat{i} - \hat{j}$ and $\hat{j} - \hat{k}$ is:

(a) $-\dfrac{\pi}{3}$
(b) $0$
(c) $\dfrac{\pi}{3}$
(d) $\dfrac{2\pi}{3}$

**Answer:** (d) $\dfrac{2\pi}{3}$

**Solution:**

Let $\vec{a} = \hat{i} - \hat{j}$ and $\vec{b} = \hat{j} - \hat{k}$.

$$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = (1)(0) + (-1)(1) + (0)(-1) = -1$$

$$|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{1^2 + (-1)^2 + 0^2} = \sqrt{2}, \quad |\vec{b}| = \sqrt{0^2 + 1^2 + (-1)^2} = \sqrt{2}$$

$$\cos\theta = \frac{\vec{a}\cdot\vec{b}}{|\vec{a}||\vec{b}|} = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}\cdot\sqrt{2}} = \frac{-1}{2}$$

$$\theta = \frac{2\pi}{3}$$

### Q9.
If $A$ is a non-singular square matrix of order 3 such that $A^2 = 3A$, then the value of $|A|$ is:

(a) $-3$
(b) $3$
(c) $9$
(d) $27$

**Answer:** (d) $27$

**Solution:**

Taking determinant on both sides of $A^2 = 3A$:

$$|A^2| = |3A|$$

$$|A|^2 = 3^3|A| = 27|A|$$

$$|A|^2 - 27|A| = 0 \implies |A|(|A| - 27) = 0$$

Since $A$ is non-singular, $|A| \neq 0$, therefore $|A| = 27$.

Find the angle between unit vectors $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$ so that $\sqrt{3}\,\vec{a} - \vec{b}$ is also a unit vector.

### Q26.
A fair dice is thrown two times. Find the probability distribution of the number of sixes. Also determine the mean of the number of sixes.

**Answer:** Mean $= \dfrac{1}{3}$

**Solution:**

Total outcomes: $6 \times 6 = 36$

| $X$ | 0 Sixes | 1 Six | 2 Sixes |
|---|---|---|---|
| $P(X)$ | $\dfrac{25}{36}$ | $\dfrac{10}{36}$ | $\dfrac{1}{36}$ |

(Outcomes with 0 sixes: $5\times5=25$; with exactly 1 six: $2\times5\times1=10$; with 2 sixes: $1$)

**Mean:**
$$E(X) = \sum X\cdot P(X) = 0\cdot\frac{25}{36} + 1\cdot\frac{10}{36} + 2\cdot\frac{1}{36} = \frac{10+2}{36} = \frac{12}{36} = \frac{1}{3}$$

### Q27.
Solve the following differential equation:

$$\left(1 - e^{y/x}\right)dy + e^{y/x}\!\left(1 - \frac{y}{x}\right)dx = 0 \quad (x \neq 0)$$

**Answer:** $y + e^{y/x}\cdot x = C$

**Solution:**

Rearranging:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{-e^{y/x}\!\left(1-\dfrac{y}{x}\right)}{1-e^{y/x}} = F(x,y)$$

**Homogeneity check:** $F(\lambda x, \lambda y) = F(x,y)$ (verified since $y/x$ is unchanged). So it is a **homogeneous DE**.

**Substitution:** Let $y = vx$, so $\dfrac{dy}{dx} = v + x\dfrac{dv}{dx}$.

$$v + x\frac{dv}{dx} = \frac{-e^v(1-v)}{1-e^v}$$

$$x\frac{dv}{dx} = \frac{-e^v + ve^v}{1-e^v} - v = \frac{-e^v + ve^v - v(1-e^v)}{1-e^v} = \frac{-e^v - v}{1-e^v}$$

Separating variables:

$$\frac{1+e^v}{v+e^v}\,dv = -\frac{dx}{x}$$

### Q30.
Check whether the relation $R$ in the set $\mathbb{N}$ of natural numbers given by $R = \{(a,b) : a \text{ is a divisor of } b\}$ is reflexive, symmetric or transitive. Also determine whether $R$ is an equivalence relation.

**Solution:**

**Reflexivity:** Every natural number $n$ divides itself, so $(n,n) \in R$ for all $n \in \mathbb{N}$.
→ $R$ is **reflexive**. ✓

**Symmetry:** Let $aRb$, i.e., $a$ divides $b$. This does not imply $b$ divides $a$.

**Transitivity:** Let $aRb$ and $bRc$. Then $a | b$ and $b | c$, which implies $a | c$, so $aRc$.
→ $R$ is **transitive**. ✓

**Conclusion:** Since $R$ is not symmetric, it is **not an equivalence relation**.
